Bring this page to every community you tour. Ask the same questions at each, jot a quick note, and compare them side by side. There are no wrong questions — a good community welcomes all of them.

The 12 questions to ask

1. What is your staff-to-resident ratio — day and overnight?
2. How are staff trained specifically in dementia care, and how often?
3. What is your staff turnover, and how long have caregivers been here?
4. How do you keep residents safe from wandering and falls?
5. How do you build a personalized care plan, and how often is it updated?
6. Can my parent stay as the disease progresses (age in place)?
7. What does a typical day look like?
8. How is dining handled for someone with dementia?
9. How do you coordinate medical care and medications?
10. How — and how often — will you communicate with our family?
11. How do you handle a difficult day without over-relying on medication?
12. What are all the costs, and what would trigger a price increase?

Trust what you observe — green flags vs. red flags

Before the tour — 10 early warning signs of dementia to discuss

If several of these are happening and don't resolve, talk to a doctor (some causes are treatable). Source: Alzheimer's Association.

  1. Memory loss that disrupts daily life
  2. Trouble planning or solving problems
  3. Difficulty completing familiar tasks
  4. Confusion with time or place
  5. Trouble with visual images & spatial relationships
  6. New problems with words
  7. Misplacing things, can't retrace steps
  8. Decreased or poor judgment
  9. Withdrawal from work or social activities
  10. Changes in mood and personality
✦ Two visits beat one

Whenever you can, visit twice — and make one stop unannounced or at mealtime. The truest picture of a community is the ordinary day you weren't expected to see.
