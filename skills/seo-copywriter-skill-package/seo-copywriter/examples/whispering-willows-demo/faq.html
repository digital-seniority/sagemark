About memory care

What is memory care? +

Memory care is a specialized type of long-term care designed for people living with Alzheimer's disease or another form of dementia. It combines a secured, calming environment with staff trained specifically in dementia care, structured daily routines, and help with everyday tasks like dressing, bathing, and medication. The goal is to keep residents safe and engaged while supporting as much independence and dignity as possible. Our guide to memory care in Skagit County is the best place to start.

What's the difference between memory care and assisted living? +

Assisted living supports older adults who need help with daily tasks but are largely independent and safe on their own. Memory care is built specifically for dementia: it adds secured entries, dementia-trained staff, a more structured routine, and a calmer environment designed to reduce confusion. Many families start in assisted living and move to memory care as dementia progresses; others choose memory care from the start when safety and supervision are the priority. We compare the two in detail in memory care vs. assisted living.

Is memory care the same as a nursing home? +

No. A nursing home (skilled nursing facility) provides round-the-clock medical and nursing care for people with serious, ongoing health conditions. Memory care is residential, home-like care focused on the day-to-day needs of people living with dementia — supervision, routine, engagement, and help with daily tasks — rather than hospital-level medical treatment. Whispering Willows is a memory care community, not a nursing home.

Who is memory care for? +

Memory care is for adults living with Alzheimer's disease or another dementia whose needs have grown beyond what can be safely managed at home or in assisted living. It often becomes the right fit when wandering, confusion, safety risks, or the demands of around-the-clock supervision begin to overwhelm family caregivers. A good memory care community supports both the resident and the family.

How do I know when it's time for memory care? +

Common signs that it may be time include wandering or getting lost, leaving the stove on or other safety incidents, increasing confusion, weight loss or missed medications, caregiver burnout, and a parent who is becoming isolated or unsafe alone. It is rarely one single moment — it is usually a pattern that builds over time. Our guide on the signs it's time for memory care walks through these in detail, and our team is glad to talk through what you're seeing.

Paying for memory care

Does Medicare pay for memory care? +

Generally, no. Medicare does not pay for the long-term, custodial care that makes up most of a memory care stay — the help with daily living and supervision that residents need over months and years. Medicare may cover short, medically necessary skilled care after a qualifying hospital stay, but not ongoing room, board, and personal care. Most families combine other sources to pay for memory care.

Does Medicaid (Apple Health) cover memory care in Washington? +

Washington's Medicaid program, Apple Health, can help pay for long-term care for those who qualify financially and medically, including care in a community setting. Coverage and eligibility rules can be complex, so it is worth reviewing the details and talking with someone who understands them. Our guide on paying for memory care explains how Medicaid fits alongside other funding sources in Washington.

Does Whispering Willows accept Medicaid? +

Yes. Whispering Willows accepts Medicaid, and we accept it without requiring a spend-down — eligible families are not required to first exhaust their assets before we can welcome their loved one. Because every family's situation is different, the simplest next step is a short phone call so we can understand your circumstances and explain your options clearly. Call us at (360) 208-7555.

How do families pay for memory care? +

Families typically pay for memory care through a mix of sources: private funds and savings, long-term care insurance, veterans' benefits such as VA Aid & Attendance for those who qualify, and Medicaid (Apple Health) for eligible families. Most families combine more than one. We're happy to help you understand which of these may apply to your situation.

How much does memory care cost? +

The honest answer depends on the level of care your loved one needs and the room type you choose, so we give every family a personalized quote by phone rather than a one-size-fits-all number. That way the figure you receive actually reflects your situation. Call (360) 208-7555 and we'll walk you through it, and our guide on paying for memory care explains the funding sources that can help.

Safety & daily life

How do you keep residents with dementia safe? +

Safety is built into everything at Whispering Willows. We have secured entries and an enclosed outdoor space so residents can enjoy fresh air without the risk of wandering, fall-prevention practices throughout the home, and dementia-trained staff on site 24 hours a day. Our aim is a setting that is genuinely secure without ever feeling like a lockdown — calm, home-like, and reassuring for residents and families alike.

What does a typical day look like? +

Days follow a gentle, predictable rhythm, which helps reduce confusion and anxiety for people living with dementia. Residents enjoy home-cooked meals, time outdoors in the enclosed garden, and engaging activities suited to their abilities and interests — music, gentle movement, conversation, and quiet moments of rest. Routines are shaped around each resident's history and preferences so the day feels familiar and comfortable.

Can my parent stay as dementia progresses (age in place)? +

In most cases, yes. Whispering Willows is designed for dementia care, and our team supports residents as their needs change over time, so families are spared another disruptive move whenever possible. Because every person's journey is different, we'll talk with you honestly about your loved one's needs and how we can meet them as things evolve.
