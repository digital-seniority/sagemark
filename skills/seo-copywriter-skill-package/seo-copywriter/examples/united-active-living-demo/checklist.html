Bring this page to every community you tour — independent living, assisted living, or memory care. Ask the same questions at each, jot a quick note, and compare them side by side. There are no wrong questions — a good community welcomes all of them.

The 12 questions to ask

1. What is your staff-to-resident ratio — day and overnight?
2. How is your team trained, and how often — and what's your staff turnover?
3. How do you keep residents safe — wandering, falls, and emergencies?
4. How do you build a personalized care plan, and how often is it updated?
5. Can my parent stay as their needs change — can they age in place?
6. What does a typical day look like — wellness, arts, and programming?
7. How is dining handled, and how do you accommodate dietary needs?
8. How do you coordinate medications and medical care?
9. How — and how often — will you communicate with our family?
10. What are all the all-in monthly costs, and what would trigger an increase?
11. How are you licensed or accredited in Alberta, and how did your last review go?
12. Do you offer a trial stay or short-term stay before we commit?

Before the tour — signs to discuss

Tours go further when you arrive clear on what your parent actually needs. Jot down what you've noticed lately — falls or near-falls, missed medications, weight loss or skipped meals, unpaid bills, isolation, or driving worries. If you're also seeing memory changes — memory loss that disrupts daily life, confusion with time or place, trouble with familiar tasks, new problems with words, withdrawal, or changes in mood — note them and talk to a doctor, as some causes are treatable. Source: Alzheimer Society of Canada.

✦ Two visits beat one

Whenever you can, visit twice — and make one stop unannounced or at mealtime. The truest picture of a community is the ordinary day you weren't expected to see.
